================================================================
                 SYNTH QUEST — OPENING CUTSCENE
                          (18 panels)
================================================================

Format per panel:
  [image]    — brief description; norns is 128x64, dithered grayscale
  [audio]    — musical cue
  [narration] — on-screen text. Max ~80 chars per line, max 3 lines.

Title appears only on the final panel. Heroes do not appear by
face in the prologue; the prologue ends as Alder begins his song.


-------- PANEL 1 --------

[image]:    Full-screen abstract: a single horizontal line of light,
            held steady across the middle of the frame. Faint
            dithering above and below suggests it is breathing.
[audio]:    Long sustained chord. Seven voices, perfectly in tune.
            No rhythm. Held.
[narration]: There was a time when the world held one chord.


-------- PANEL 2 --------

[image]:    The same line, now subtly thicker. Around it, faint
            shapes resolve into mountains, rivers, towers — a
            world drawn out of the chord itself.
[audio]:    The chord continues. Soft pad textures wash under it.
[narration]: It was called Modalia. Its mountains, its rivers,
             its people: each a voice in a single sustained song.


-------- PANEL 3 --------

[image]:    Tighter on the chord-line. A small symbol at its center
            — a stylized Crystal Synth, seven facets joined.
[audio]:    The chord, foregrounded. The seven voices heard
            separately, then folding back into one.
[narration]: At its heart sang the Crystal Synth.
             One instrument. Seven voices. The held chord of the world.


-------- PANEL 4 --------

[image]:    The Crystal Synth symbol, alone on black. A hairline
            crack appears across it.
[audio]:    The chord falters. One voice slips a quarter-tone.
[narration]: No one remembers what broke it.


-------- PANEL 5 --------

[image]:    The Crystal Synth shatters into seven pieces. Each
            shard a different shape; they drift apart slowly
            against a black field.
[audio]:    The chord splits. Seven voices, each going its own way,
            each holding its own pitch as it drifts.
[narration]: Seven shards fell.
             One for each voice the world had been singing.


-------- PANEL 6 --------

[image]:    A simple map of Modalia, drawn in line. Seven small
            light-points scattered across it where the shards
            came to rest.
[audio]:    Seven separate drones, each in its own mode, layered
            but no longer aligned.
[narration]: Seven shards, seven modes, seven nations.
             Lydian. Dorian. Mixolydian.
             Phrygian. Aeolian. Locrian. Ionian.


-------- PANEL 7 --------

[image]:    A pan across the map. Tiny pixel scenes by each light:
            a village, a tower, a temple, a port, a cliffside city.
[audio]:    Each drone briefly foregrounded as the camera passes
            it, like tuning across a band.
[narration]: An age went by.
             Each nation kept its shard. Each sang in its own mode.
             The world was diminished. But it was alive.


-------- PANEL 8 --------

[image]:    A region of the map between two lights — wider than
            it should be. Empty. The dithering thins to nothing.
[audio]:    A pad drops out. The remaining drones thin.
[narration]: Between the nations the music thinned.
             Travellers learned to cross the silent country
             by ear, not by map.


-------- PANEL 9 --------

[image]:    A new light at the edge of the map. Not warm. A pinprick
            of cold light at the top of a distant tower.
[audio]:    A low, suppressive hum. Not loud. It lowers the noise
            floor of the room.
[narration]: Then, in the quiet places, a new voice rose.


-------- PANEL 10 --------

[image]:    A figure in silhouette at the high window of the tower.
            Featureless. Tall. Holding nothing.
[audio]:    The hum continues. The other drones, faintly, recede.
[narration]: He called himself Suno.
             The last syllable of song.
             What is left when the music is gone.


-------- PANEL 11 --------

[image]:    The tower window, brighter. Around its base, columns
            of smoke or sound rising from machinery.
[audio]:    A mechanical pulse joins the hum. Industrial. Steady.
            Not musical.
[narration]: He did not want the shards to wield.
             He wanted them silenced.
             Cast into the space between notes.


-------- PANEL 12 --------

[image]:    The map again. One of the seven lights has gone dim.
            Around it, the dithering thins outward — the land is
            going flat.
[audio]:    One of the drones drops out entirely. The remaining
            voices sound smaller for it.
[narration]: One shard had already gone quiet.
             The land that held it began to forget itself.


-------- PANEL 13 --------

[image]:    Map zoomed: the dimming spreads slowly outward from
            the dead shard, eating into a neighbouring nation.
[audio]:    A second voice begins to flatten by a half-step.
            It does not drop out — it goes slightly, terribly,
            wrong.
[narration]: The flatness moved like weather.
             Children born under it sang half a step low
             and could not say why.


-------- PANEL 14 --------

[image]:    The remaining six shard-lights pulse together — a
            slow simultaneous flicker. As if they have noticed.
[audio]:    The six drones brighten for one bar, then settle.
[narration]: The shards heard him coming.


-------- PANEL 15 --------

[image]:    Pull back to the full map. One of the lights — Lydian
            country — pulses softer than the others. Beside it,
            a small pixel cluster: a clearing, a stone inn, a
            handful of figures.
[audio]:    The Lydian drone foregrounded. Warm. Suspended.
[narration]: Far from his tower, in a small clearing,
             on an evening that did not yet know it was changing,
             an audience gathered.


-------- PANEL 16 --------

[image]:    A closer pixel scene of the Village Clearing at dusk.
            A handful of seated figures: an elder, two children,
            a robed woman in finer cloth than the rest. A figure
            with a lute is rising to play, back to camera.
[audio]:    The drone fades. A single lute string, tuned.
[narration]: A few elders. A few children. A visiting princess.
             A young bard, paid in coin and supper,
             tuning a lute he had borrowed from a friend.


-------- PANEL 17 --------

[image]:    Closer still. The bard's hand on the lute, mid-strum.
            Outside the clearing, in the trees, a glint of metal:
            something watching.
[audio]:    The lute begins. One bar — bright Lydian arpeggio,
            warm and unaware. Behind it, almost too quiet to
            register, the suppressive hum from earlier.
[narration]: He began to play.
             He had no idea who was listening from the trees.


-------- PANEL 18 --------

[image]:    Title card. Bold pixel text centered: SYNTH QUEST.
            A small Crystal Synth glyph below the title, with
            two of its seven facets faintly lit.
[audio]:    Title theme — short FF1-Prelude inspired arpeggios,
            ascending. Two voices warm.
[narration]: SYNTH QUEST
             (no further text)
