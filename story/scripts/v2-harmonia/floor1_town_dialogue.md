# Floor 1 — HEMMELFELD-CLOISTER — Town NPC Dialogue

*Hemmelfeld region. The first town. A stone village around a domed cloister, bell-orchards in concentric rings. The Triangle Hymn bleeds from the cloister at all times — a soft triangle pad, slight chorus, long hall reverb, distant bells. At dusk in the prologue the round is faltering: voices drop in and out, the bells half-toll. Tape saturation runs constant beneath everything.*

*Compress every line for the norns dialogue box: ~25 chars × 3 lines, or ~30 chars × 2 lines. Tone per bible §9 — old-world cadence, earnest, not parody.*

---

## NPC: HYMN-MOTHER MAREN OYE
**Role:** Bren's older sister; senior Cleric of the cloister; the one who dispatches the party.
**Location:** at the cloister door, robe still dust-marked from the chapel below.
**Voice/timbre:** soft low triangle, dry, almost no chorus — the still center of the round.
**Ambient cue:** the Hymn audibly thins as you approach her. one voice missing, then briefly returning.

### First interaction
"You have heard the Hymn
falter, brother. So have I.
Do not pretend otherwise."

[A again]
"The Crystal under the
chapel has gone dim.
The round cannot hold it."

[A again]
"Take the relics. Take
the road. Find the cause.
Do not stop until it sings."

### Repeat interaction (no quest active)
"The Hymn does not wait
for any one of us. That
is the kindness of it."

[A again]
"Go, Bren. I will hold
your part of the round
until you return."

### Quest hook
**Quest title:** *The Faltering Hymn* (Floor 1 main quest — begins the Crystal of Triangle arc)
**Trigger:** speaking to Maren after the prologue cutscene resolves; party of four assembled in the square.

"Beneath the cloister there
is a chapel none of us
have entered in our lives."

"The dimming began there.
The Hymn-Mothers before me
sealed it. I am unsealing it."

> [Accept] / [Not yet]

[Accept]
"Then the round is yours
to mend. Go down. Sing it
back. Bring me a whole tone."

[Not yet]
"Wait, then. But the Hymn
is thinning, brother. Wait
gently. Do not wait long."

### Quest progress
"The chapel is open. I can
hear it from here. Be quick.
Be in tune."

### Quest complete
"You sang it back. I felt
the round close. The
Crystal is breathing again."

"Take the road east. The
Arx will know more than I
do. Travel in tune, brother."

## NPC: OLD FERRO
**Role:** the oldest singer in the round; keeper of the lost second verse.
**Location:** on the porch of the round-house, both hands flat on his knees.
**Voice/timbre:** an almost-vanished triangle, half-eaten by tape hiss; the wow on his vowels is audible.
**Ambient cue:** the master tape's hiss is louder around him than anywhere else in the village.

### First interaction
"You want to know the
second verse."

[A again]
"Nobody's sung it since
the year my father stopped.
The second verse is for stopping."

[A again]
"Sit a while. Hum the first
with me. Sometimes the second
remembers itself for me. Sometimes."

### Repeat interaction (no quest active)
"Eyes closed. Listening.
The Hymn is a half-step thin
today. You hear it too?"

[A again]
"When I was small the round
was sung in three parts.
It was thinner then. We were braver."

### Quest hook
**Quest title:** *The Second Verse* (side quest — musical reward)
**Trigger:** after the Faltering Hymn quest is accepted, but only if the player has spoken to Ferro twice.

"There is a verse in me I
cannot reach alone. I need
a voice that scans."

"Bring me the bard, the
one who came in last night.
Lir. They have the listening pitch."

> [I will] / [Not now]

[I will]
"Good. Sit them on the
porch beside me. We will
hum until the gap fills."

[Not now]
"Go on, then. The verse
will wait. It has waited
longer than you have lived."

### Quest progress
"Bring the bard. My voice
alone is not enough to
remember for both of us."

### Quest complete
"There. The second verse.
Carry it east with you.
Sing it where stopping is needed."

[reward: *Second Verse* — a battle-use item that lets the party safely end an encounter with a Round-Wraith without combat]

## NPC: THE ROUND-MASTER
**Role:** the sustained low triangle root of the Hymn; a woman who has given up her name for the post.
**Location:** standing in the chapel antechamber, eyes closed, holding her note. she does not stop holding it while she speaks.
**Voice/timbre:** a low triangle drone underneath every word — she speaks *over* her own root, never breaking it.
**Ambient cue:** her root is the loudest pitched tone in the village; standing near her, every other sound tunes to it.

### First interaction
"I do not have a name.
The root is my name now.
Speak quickly."

[A again]
"You hold a cylinder
in your bedroll, Cleric.
I can hear it not breathing."

[A again]
"I will not tell your sister.
But I will not teach you the
middle register for nothing."

### Repeat interaction (no quest active)
"My root is steady today.
The valley is grateful. So
am I. Go on."

[A again]
"Listen without speaking
for a moment. There. That
is the Hymn. It is enough."

### Quest hook
**Quest title:** *The Middle Register* (side quest — Cleric stat boost / unlocks Bren's chorale ability tier)
**Trigger:** speak to her with Bren in the lead; only available after the prologue has placed the cylinder in his inventory.

"Play it for me. Once.
The wax of your mother.
Here. With me listening."

"I will not let it leave
this room. You will not
play it again until you must."

> [Play it] / [Not yet]

[Play it]
"...Thank you. She sang
the third part. I had
forgotten. Sit. I will teach you."

[Not yet]
"Then come back when you
can bear it. The middle
register is not for the unready."

### Quest progress
"I am waiting, Cleric.
The cylinder will not get
lighter in your bedroll."

### Quest complete
"You have the middle now.
Use it sparingly. A voice
between two voices is a bridge."

[reward: Bren learns *Middle Register* — a chorale ability that holds two enemy voices in tune simultaneously, halving an Imperial Chorister's cadence build]

## NPC: TILDE THE BELL-PRUNER
**Role:** caretaker of the bell-orchards in the second concentric ring; tunes the bronze fruit by ear each spring.
**Location:** up a ladder in a bell-tree at the village edge, tuning hammer at her belt.
**Voice/timbre:** clean triangle with a struck-bell attack — short ping at the top of each phrase, then warm pad.
**Ambient cue:** when she speaks, two or three of her bells hum sympathetically in the tree above her.

### First interaction
"Mind your head. Three
of these went sour
in the night."

[A again]
"They were in tune at dusk.
By dawn they were a quarter
flat. Bells are not supposed to drift."

[A again]
"Something under the
cloister is pulling. I can
hear it through the bronze."

### Repeat interaction
"Up here a moment. The
fourth ring needs a half-
turn. Go on past me."

[A again]
"If a bell will not tune,
you do not blame the bell.
You ask what it is hearing."

### Quest hook
**Quest title:** *The Sour Three* (side quest — relic reward)
**Trigger:** approach her after speaking to Maren about the chapel.

"Three bells. Three names
on their clappers. Strike
each one. Tell me what they say."

"I cannot leave the orchard.
The fourth ring will go sour
if I climb down. Please."

> [I will] / [Not now]

[I will]
"Bless you. The names are
worn. Bring the sounds back,
not the words."

[Not now]
"The bells will wait. They
are made of waiting. Come
back when your road allows."

### Quest progress
"The first ring. The third.
The seventh. Strike them
soft. They are tired."

### Quest complete
"You heard them. Good. Take
this. It was on the inside
of the seventh's clapper."

[reward: *Tuning Slug* — a small lead weight inscribed with a forgotten name; equip on Bren or Aldine for +1 to all sustained-note checks; lore item — the name belonged to the Hymn-Mother three before Maren]

## NPC: THE POST-RIDER, JESSEM
**Role:** the courier whose coach brought Aldine into Hemmelfeld at dusk; he has not unhitched the Metronome yet.
**Location:** at the village square's post-stake, holding the bridle of a tired Metronome whose arm is still swinging in tempo.
**Voice/timbre:** a square voice, a little out of tune with the village; the Metronome's tick is audible behind him on every other word.
**Ambient cue:** the Metronome's pendulum-arm clicks on the bar; if you stand still, you fall into its tempo.

### First interaction
"From the Arx in three days.
The roads are quiet. Too quiet.
That is not a figure of speech."

[A again]
"There were three towns I
passed where no one was
singing. Not even to themselves."

[A again]
"The provost sent the small
woman in spectacles. Whatever
he wrote her, he wrote it twice."

### Repeat interaction
"The Metronome will not
rest yet. She is still
in tempo. I am waiting her out."

[A again]
"You ride east, you ride at
her pace. Not yours. Make
your peace with that early."

### Quest hook
*(none — Jessem gives no quest. He is here because the world has roads, and the roads have riders, and one of those riders just brought the plot to your door.)*

## NPC: CRINNA, A CHILD IN THE CHOIR
**Role:** seven years old; one of the youngest voices in the round, just learning her part.
**Location:** sitting on the cloister wall, kicking her heels, *not* singing — she has been told to rest her voice.
**Voice/timbre:** a small clean triangle, a half-step under pitch (per the bible's §1 — children born this season).
**Ambient cue:** when she sings a fragment, the Hymn around her bends slightly toward her flatness, then corrects.

### First interaction
"I am not allowed to sing
right now. Hymn-Mother
says my pitch is wrong."

[A again]
"It is not wrong. I am
*singing* it right. The
Hymn is the one that moved."

[A again]
"You believe me, don't
you? You came from far.
You can hear it too."

### Repeat interaction
"I am still not singing.
I am waiting. I am very
good at waiting now."

[A again]
"When you fix it, will my
voice come back up to where
the others are? Or will theirs come down?"

### Quest hook
*(no formal quest, but on quest complete of The Faltering Hymn she has one closing line — see Maren's quest complete state. If the player returns to Crinna after restoring the Crystal:)*

"Listen. Listen. I am
on pitch again. I did
not have to move."

"You moved the world
back. Thank you."
