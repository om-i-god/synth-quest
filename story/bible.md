This document supersets the dossier. Where the dossier locks a fact,
this bible defers to it ("see Dossier §..."). Where the dossier is
silent or marks something TBD, this bible proposes.

Anything labeled `STATUS: PROPOSED` is a sketch and not committed.
Anything labeled `(planned)` is on the roadmap but not built. The
playable shape today is Village Clearing + Hollow Woods, with the
Lydian and Dorian shards recovered. See Dossier §SHARDS — STATUS.


-------- THE WORLD: MODALIA --------

Modalia is a planet whose physical laws lean on sound the way ours
lean on light. Plants grow toward tone. Weather follows interval.
A land's music is not a metaphor for its character — it is the
substance of it. Pull the music out of a region and the wheat goes
tasteless, the rivers slow, the children forget their own names.

The lifeblood of all this is the Crystal Synth (see Dossier §CRYSTAL
SYNTH). What follows is what the Crystal Synth was, and what is
remembered of the time before it broke.

The Held Chord.
   Before the shattering, the Crystal Synth sang as one — seven
   modes braided into a single sustained chord that held the planet
   in tune. Modalians call that age the HELD CHORD. Almost no one
   alive today has heard it; it is known the way our own world
   "knows" the songs of extinct birds. Bards in every nation keep
   fragments — eight bars here, a cadence there — but no one has
   the whole. Some scholars (Diegues among them) argue that the
   Held Chord is not lost so much as scattered: every folk-tune
   in every nation is a ghost of one of its voices.

Origin of the Synth.
   The dossier does not commit on who made the Crystal Synth, and
   neither does this bible. Three traditions exist in Modalia, none
   of them official:
     (a) The Synth made itself — the first sound, condensed.
     (b) A vanished people sang it into existence and then went
         quiet inside it. (This tradition is closest to the Act 4
         truth; see Dossier §ARC.)
     (c) The Synth is older than the planet and the planet grew
         around it the way moss grows around a struck bell.
   The game does not need to choose. Suno believes (a) — that the
   Synth is just a thing, a found object, breakable. Diegues leans
   toward (b) but won't say so out loud. Lyrik in the Village
   Clearing speaks as if (c) were obvious.

The Shattering.
   The dossier marks the cause as "lost to the deep past." Treat
   that as canon. We show the effect, not the cause: a long single
   tone sustained, then seven notes scattering. Whatever broke the
   Synth, the world's reaction was the same — the chord opened, the
   tone-color of the world thinned, and seven shards fell into seven
   places. Each place that received a shard slowly grew a culture
   around the mode that shard sang.

Geography.
   Modalia is a single continent ringed by a sea. The seven nations
   are not evenly spaced — they cluster around the shards' resting
   places, which means the planet's political map is geological
   rather than designed. Between the nations lie the DEAD LANDS:
   regions the Held Chord once reached but no shard now sustains.
   In the Dead Lands the music is flat by half a step, then by a
   whole step further in, then atonal at the deepest waste. Travelers
   cross them by ear, not by map. Suno's reach extends fastest
   through these waste corridors; nothing in them is loud enough
   to push his quiet back.

The Village Clearing sits at the western edge of Lydian country,
near the Dead Lands border. The Hollow Woods are on the eastern
slope, where Lydian softens into Dorian. The other five nations
lie further out (see THE SEVEN NATIONS, below).


-------- THE SEVEN NATIONS --------

Two nations are partially established by current play. Five are
proposals.


-------- 1. LYDIAN — THE CLEARING LANDS --------

   STATUS: ESTABLISHED (Village Clearing + adjacent regions)

   Mode character: bright, suspended, the sharpened fourth that
   never quite resolves. Lifts. Floats. Refuses to settle.

   Culture: village agrarian, troupe-based. Music here is communal,
   often pentatonic, sung at festivals and harvests. The Lydian
   shard sleeps in a cave east of the Village Clearing (Cave 1).
   Recovered (see Dossier §BESTIARY, Cave Echo).

   Aesthetic: greens and clear sky, stone inn, dirt paths, lutes
   strung loose, fiddles, hand drums. People are mostly unguarded.
   They have never had to be.

   FF reference: FF9 Dali / Lindblum outskirts — a place a young
   bard could realistically grow up unnoticed.


-------- 2. DORIAN — THE HOLLOW WOODS REACH --------

   STATUS: ESTABLISHED (Hollow Woods + adjacent regions)

   Mode character: minor with a raised sixth — wistful but forward-
   leaning. The mode of journeys. Marching but mournful.

   Culture: forest-dwellers, wood-wise, half-monastic. Veris's
   order keeps the wood. Music here is modal-folk: drone strings
   and slow-bowed melodies. The Dorian shard slept in Cave 2,
   under the Forest Sentinel; recovered.

   Aesthetic: deep greens, fog, lichen, standing stones, cabins
   built into the trunks of vast trees.

   FF reference: FF9 Black Mage Village / Cleyra's roots.


-------- 3. MIXOLYDIAN — TBD --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED

   Mode character: major with flat seventh. Sunlit folksong. The
   mode of dance, of taverns, of the road home.

   Proposed nation: a sun-bleached coastal stretch of fishing towns
   and ports. Long days, long evenings, music played outdoors.
   Climate warm and dry. Trial-temple is an open-air amphitheatre
   built into a cliff face — the boss is a piece of music played
   live by a competing bard-king who refuses to give up the shard
   without a duel of song. Possible boss: HARBORMASTER, an old
   bandleader whose attacks are call-and-response phrases the
   party must "answer" on the correct beat.

   FF reference: FF5 Walse / FF9 Treno's gentler face.


-------- 4. PHRYGIAN — TBD --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED

   Mode character: minor with flat second. Tense, exotic, ritual.
   Spanish in our world. Eastern in Modalia.

   Proposed nation: a high-desert city of sand-coloured towers and
   night markets. Music is microtonal, drone-based, vocal. Climate
   hot by day, freezing by night. Trial-temple is a buried palace
   under shifting dunes; entry shifts each cycle. Possible boss:
   THE FIRST CALL, a giant ritual horn that the party fights as a
   structure rather than a creature — they damage it by interrupting
   its phrases.

   FF reference: FF6 Figaro / FF7 Cosmo Canyon, with FF9 Cleyra's
   ritual edge.


-------- 5. AEOLIAN — TBD --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED

   Mode character: natural minor. Wistful. The mode of memory and
   loss. Miel's home register.

   Proposed nation: a sea-cliff capital city, white stone, pale
   blue water, long cold winters. Court culture: chamber music,
   sung verse, formal mourning songs. The mother country of Princess
   Miel (per Dossier §PARTY — Miel is from "the capital"; this bible
   commits her capital to Aeolian country). Trial-temple is the
   royal cathedral itself; the shard lives in the foundation. Per
   Dossier §ARC, this nation is the one Suno breaks in Act 3 to
   trigger the World of Silence — the temple is the site of the
   catastrophe. Possible boss (Act 3 phase): THE BROKEN CADENCE,
   a former cathedral chorister now playing Suno's instrument.

   FF reference: FF10 Bevelle / FF9 Alexandria. Miel as Garnet/Yuna.


-------- 6. LOCRIAN — TBD --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED

   Mode character: diminished. The broken seventh. The only mode
   that resists settling.

   Proposed nation: a half-ruined region on the edge of the Dead
   Lands. The shard's instability has been bleeding into the soil
   for centuries; the people who remain are stoic, half-deafened,
   sing in cracked harmony. Climate: ash, twilight, low cloud.
   Trial-temple is a half-collapsed observatory whose architecture
   is itself a tritone — every angle wrong on purpose. Possible
   boss: THE TRITONE, a creature whose every attack is two notes
   that refuse to resolve until the party plays the resolving third.

   FF reference: FF6 Kefka's Tower's approach corridor / FF7's
   Sleeping Forest at its strangest.


-------- 7. IONIAN — TBD --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED

   Mode character: pure major. The cadence home. The mode of
   triumph and arrival.

   Proposed nation: a lost or hidden country — by Act 4 the party
   learns it is the vanished nation that sang Alder into being
   (see Dossier §ARC, Act 4). Geography: an island, or a place that
   shifts, or a city beneath the sea — undetermined. Climate:
   eternal first light. Trial-temple may be the Crystal Synth itself
   re-entered. Possible boss: none in the conventional sense — the
   Ionian shard may be granted, not won. (Or: it must be sung into
   place by the party, with no enemy at all, the "battle" being
   the world's last act of remembering.)

   FF reference: FF9 Memoria / FF10 Zanarkand. Held until last.


-------- THE PARTY — DEEPER --------

For dossier-canon roles, voices, and inspirations, see Dossier
§PARTY. What follows is the deeper read.


-------- ALDER (Bard / Protagonist) --------

   Stat block (level 1, proposed):
      HP 120   MP  30   ATK 5   DEF 4   MAG 4   SPD 2
   Stat shape: a generalist who tilts toward MAG and SPD as he
   levels. Never the strongest hitter, never the toughest. The
   binding voice.

   Mode affinity (proposed): Alder is unaligned at the start —
   he is the song that holds others, not a song of his own. His
   affinity wakes shard by shard. By Act 4 the affinity reveals
   itself: he is Ionian. The cadence home. He doesn't know it
   until the world tells him.

   Voice in the ensemble:
      Alder's wavetable scan reads as a young man who hasn't yet
      decided what kind of singer he is. The filter sweep on his
      PLAY action lifts the whole party for one bar — that lift
      is Alder's signature, audible even in fights where he hasn't
      attacked once. When he is low on HP the wavetable narrows
      toward a single sine and the filter closes; when he is healed
      it opens again. The party can hear how he is doing without
      looking.

   Inspiration (per Dossier): FF9 ZIDANE.


-------- MIEL (Cleric / Princess) --------

   Stat block (level 1, proposed):
      HP 100   MP  60   ATK 3   DEF 5   MAG 6   SPD 1
   Stat shape: low SPD by design — she heals on whole notes, a
   long slow respiration in the middle of the fight. Highest MP
   pool of the four. The lowest ATK; she has never struck anyone
   in her life.

   Mode affinity (proposed): Aeolian. Her healing songs are in
   natural minor; she was raised on them. Her family register.
   When she heals in Aeolian country in Act 3, the heals overshoot
   slightly — overhealing as a small mechanic of grief.

   Voice in the ensemble:
      Triangle wave with chorus. The chorus is the choir she grew
      up inside; her voice is never quite alone. Sustained envelope
      reads as a held breath rather than an attack. When she dies
      in battle the chorus vanishes immediately and the room goes
      drier — every other voice in the mix loses its small wash of
      depth. The team feels her absence sonically before mechanically.

   Inspiration (per Dossier): FF9 GARNET / DAGGER + FF10 YUNA.


-------- STROM (Warrior / Defected Commander) --------

   Stat block (level 1, proposed):
      HP 200   MP  10   ATK 8   DEF 8   MAG 1   SPD 3
   Stat shape: huge HP and DEF, almost no MAG. He is the wall.
   SPD moderate (3) — he can BLK reliably without losing whole
   bars to wind-up.

   Mode affinity (proposed): Phrygian, learned in service. His
   old company drilled to Phrygian war chants; the affinity is
   a scar he has not yet untangled from his identity. His arc
   touches him toward Dorian — the journeying, redemptive minor.
   By the end he plays in both at once.

   Voice in the ensemble:
      Pulse + sub sine, short envelope. The pulse width is fixed
      — he is not flexible. Every hit lands on the bar and shakes
      the bottom of the mix. When BLK absorbs damage for another
      member, the redirected hit lands on Strom's voice instead of
      theirs — the listener hears the hit move across the stereo
      field, from the targeted voice into Strom's. That is his
      atonement made audible.

   Inspiration (per Dossier): FF4 CECIL.


-------- DIEGUES (Mage / Scholar) --------

   Stat block (level 1, proposed):
      HP  90   MP  50   ATK 3   DEF 3   MAG 9   SPD 4
   Stat shape: glass cannon. Highest MAG, second-highest MP,
   lowest HP after Miel. SPD = 4 means quarter-note casts —
   the engine of the party's damage output.

   Mode affinity (proposed): Lydian. The mathematical mode, the
   raised fourth — Diegues is at home in Lydian country and his
   damage scales there before anywhere else. He is the only party
   member who can read the Lydian shard's notation directly; in
   later acts other members start asking him to translate.

   Voice in the ensemble:
      2-op FM bell. Sine carrier, sine modulator, soft LP. His
      hit is brief and precise — a struck bell that decays cleanly
      and does not interfere with anyone else's sustain. He is the
      voice that says "this is true." When he speaks in cutscenes
      his lines should land like that bell: short, exact, leaving
      the room a little quieter.

   Inspiration (per Dossier): FF9 VIVI + FF6 TERRA.


-------- SUNO AND HIS COURT --------

For Suno's basic shape, see Dossier §ANTAGONIST. What follows is
the deeper structure.

The industrialization of music.
   Suno does not destroy shards directly. He cannot — not yet.
   What he does instead is REFINE them. His towers are full of
   apparatus that takes a stolen shard, splits its mode into raw
   harmonic content, and pumps that content into amplifiers, sirens,
   and weapons. A refined shard powers a Suno-instrument for years.
   When the shard is finally exhausted it goes mute — and that
   muted shard is what Suno casts into the Silence. The world reads
   this as "destruction"; mechanically it is a slow industrial
   bleed-out.

   The parallel is intentionally Shinra-like (Dossier §RECURRING
   THEMES). The cost is not metaphorical: the lands a refined shard
   once protected go flat-pitch, then atonal, then dead. By the
   start of the game two shards' worth of land are already silent
   wastes — those are the Dead Lands. The party will see refineries
   in person from Act 2 on.

The shape of his power.
   Suno's own voice is not loud. It is suppressive. He lowers the
   noise floor of the room he is in; every other voice in his
   presence has to work harder. In combat (final-act, planned) his
   theme does not add layers — it subtracts them. Every phase
   change in the Suno fight strips a voice from the battle theme.
   The last phase has no music at all.

The lieutenants (proposed — three named).

   1. KAEL OF THE SECOND VOICE
      STATUS: PROPOSED — Act 2 antagonist
      A coloratura-trained vocalist who lost her voice in Suno's
      service and now wears a mask whose throat is a tuned reed.
      She speaks through Suno's apparatus. Sonic signature: a
      pitched-down, ring-modulated soprano. Her arc beat: she was
      Miel's vocal teacher in Aeolian country, exiled after a
      scandal nobody remembers correctly. The Act 3 catastrophe
      is partly her revenge.

   2. THE QUARTERMASTER
      STATUS: PROPOSED — recurring antagonist, Acts 2–3
      Strom's former second-in-command. A heavy, methodical man
      who marches in Phrygian. He believes Strom defected out of
      cowardice and pursues him through the early acts. Sonic
      signature: a single low square pulse on every bar, no
      variation. His arc beat: he is the mirror Strom must face;
      they fight twice, and the second fight is winnable only if
      Strom blocks for him. (Sketch.)

   3. CONDUCTOR ARSEN
      STATUS: PROPOSED — Act 3–4 antagonist
      The engineer of the Aeolian catastrophe. A small man, polite,
      who wears earplugs at all times and keeps a metronome in his
      pocket. He runs the largest refinery in the silenced lands.
      Sonic signature: he has no voice of his own — only the click
      of the metronome and the hum of his machines. He is the
      industrialization made human-sized. Diegues respects him
      almost against his will.

The Act 4 reveal — preserved in shape, not in spoiler.
   Per Dossier §ARC Act 4: Alder and Suno share an origin neither
   yet understands. They are two notes of one chord. This bible
   does not commit any new specifics beyond the dossier — the
   reveal is held in trust. The bible only notes that everything
   Suno does in Acts 1–3 should retroactively read as a man
   destroying himself.


-------- THE FIVE-ACT ARC, EXPANDED --------

   STATUS: ACT 1 IN DEVELOPMENT; ACTS 2–5 PLANNED.

For the loglines, see Dossier §ARC. What follows is the expanded
read for each act.


-------- ACT 1 — THE SHATTERED CALLING --------

   Loglines: Dossier §ARC, Act 1.

   Foregrounded: ALDER. Miel as catalyst, Strom as defector,
   Diegues as guide. The act is Alder's act because the act ends
   with him deciding to leave home.

   Shape: Alder performs at the Village Clearing. Suno's scouts
   raid mid-performance; the clearing scatters. Alder flees east
   with Princess Miel. In the Hollow Woods they encounter Diegues
   already studying the local shard, and the three are ambushed
   by a Suno patrol led by Strom — who, mid-fight, stops, sees
   what he has been ordered to do, and turns. The four enter the
   cave together. Cave 1 (Lydian) and Cave 2 (Dorian) are recovered
   in close succession. (Note: the dossier flags that current
   prototype starts the four together; staged recruitment is a
   future refactor. This bible accepts that.)

   Midpoint twist: Strom recognises the patrol commander he replaces
   in Cave 2 — the Forest Sentinel is wearing an old Suno-issued
   harness under the moss. The Sentinel is a corrupted predecessor.
   Strom's first act of penance is to lay it to rest properly.

   Emotional beats:
      - Alder's first time outside his troupe.
      - Miel walking the woods barefoot for the first time.
      - Strom's first words to anyone who isn't his commander.
      - Diegues meeting people who actually listen to him.

   New mechanics / instruments unlocked:
      - Lute (Alder, default)
      - Holy verse (Miel, healing call)
      - Heavy block (Strom)
      - Bell-cast (Diegues, FM)

   Music layer shifts:
      - Title theme established.
      - Overworld theme begins as a thin two-voice line. By the
        end of Act 1, recovering Lydian and Dorian shards adds two
        voices to the overworld theme — the world is louder than
        when the act began.


-------- ACT 2 — THE SEVEN NATIONS --------

   Loglines: Dossier §ARC, Act 2.

   Foregrounded: ENSEMBLE. No single hero takes the act; it's the
   travel act, and each member gets a short solo arc per nation
   visited.

   Shape: a pilgrimage. The party crosses the Dead Lands, recovers
   shards from the remaining nations — the order is open, but
   Mixolydian is most likely first (gentle, social, an easy reentry
   after the cave acts), then Phrygian, then Aeolian. (Locrian and
   Ionian held for later acts.) Each nation grants a memory of its
   lost time; this is the Junction-style mechanic noted in the
   dossier.

   Midpoint twist: KAEL OF THE SECOND VOICE shows herself in
   Aeolian country. Miel recognises her. Miel does not say so.

   Emotional beats:
      - Alder ↔ Miel romance grows in side dialogue per dossier.
      - Diegues, in Lydian/Mixolydian country, is for the first
        time in a place that doesn't think he's strange.
      - Strom is recognised by old comrades in two nations and
        has to decide each time whether to fight or to ask.

   New mechanics / instruments unlocked:
      - Resonances (see SUMMONS section, planned).
      - Mode-attunement: party gains small stat boosts in nations
        whose mode they are affined to.

   Music layer shifts:
      - Each recovered shard adds one voice to the overworld theme.
      - Refinery zones are heard before they are seen — the music
        flatlines a half-step.


-------- ACT 3 — THE WORLD OF SILENCE --------

   Loglines: Dossier §ARC, Act 3.

   Foregrounded: MIEL — and her absence.

   Shape: Suno's forces strike Aeolian country (Miel's homeland).
   Conductor Arsen detonates the cathedral refinery. The Aeolian
   shard's tonal center fractures and the world goes atonal —
   pitch loses meaning across the entire planet for several in-game
   days. Miel is taken (or apparently killed) in the catastrophe.
   The party crosses the broken world nation by nation, restoring
   tonality at each shard-temple. The act ends with Miel recovered.

   Midpoint twist: when the world goes atonal the overworld theme
   does NOT cut out. It plays — but as pitch-randomized noise. The
   melody is still there, structurally; it has lost only its
   tuning. This is the player's first hint that pitch is the
   surface of music in Modalia, not the substance.

   Emotional beats:
      - Alder's first real grief.
      - Strom and Diegues, alone with him for a long stretch.
      - The reunion is short and almost wordless. Miel is changed.

   New mechanics / instruments unlocked:
      - Atonal-mode combat: enemies' attack pitches randomize.
        The party must compensate by listening, not watching.
      - "Tuned region" overlays: the player carries a small bubble
        of restored pitch with them as they travel.

   Music layer shifts:
      - The overworld theme loses three of its voices for most
        of Act 3. Each restored nation returns one.
      - The battle theme drops its drone. The fights feel naked.


-------- ACT 4 — THE TRUTH --------

   Loglines: Dossier §ARC, Act 4.

   Foregrounded: ALDER. The act is Alder's identity act.

   Shape: per the dossier, Alder learns he was sung into existence
   by a now-vanished nation as the answer to Suno; he and Suno are
   two notes of one chord; Suno is bleeding back from a future
   where he already won. The cycle has happened before.

   Midpoint twist: Diegues finds the score. Literally — a written
   piece of music that contains both Alder and Suno as parts. He
   realises he's been transcribing Alder's PLAY action without
   knowing why it was familiar.

   Emotional beats:
      - Alder asking Miel whether she still wants him knowing
        what he is.
      - Strom telling Alder he doesn't care what he is.
      - Diegues, characteristically, only speaks once in the act,
        and it's the line that breaks Alder open.

   New mechanics / instruments unlocked:
      - Alder's wavetable shows a hidden second register — Ionian.
      - The overworld theme reveals it has been in Ionian's
        relative key the whole time.

   Music layer shifts:
      - For the first time, Alder's voice can sustain like Miel's.


-------- ACT 5 — THE FINAL SILENCE --------

   Loglines: Dossier §ARC, Act 5.

   Foregrounded: ENSEMBLE, but the camera ends on Miel.

   Shape: per the dossier — final dungeon at the Crystal Synth's
   heart, Suno revealed as not the true antagonist but the latest
   carrier of the Silence, Miel's sacrifice option, Alder's
   alternative. Alder returns to the Synth as song. Modalia is
   restored. Miel is queen of a healed world.

   Midpoint twist: the Silence is not a creature. It is a missing
   voice in the chord. The fight against it is the party SINGING
   the missing voice for a sustained period — not winning by
   damage, winning by holding the note.

   Emotional beats:
      - Alder's last performance for the troupe (cameo of Village
        Clearing NPCs in the audience, if achievable).
      - Miel's coronation, scored sparsely.
      - Strom and Diegues at the gate of the Synth, not entering.

   Music layer shifts:
      - The overworld theme, for the first time, plays in full
        seven-voice form. Briefly. Then settles into something
        smaller, livable.


-------- RESONANCES --------

   STATUS: PROPOSED — system not yet implemented.

In-fiction name: RESONANCES. (Considered: Echoes, Hymns, Voices.
Resonances chosen because the word in Modalia means "a sound that
keeps singing after its source has gone.")

A Resonance is a named entity that the party can call into the
song mid-fight. Each Resonance is a famous instrument or technique
remembered in Modalian myth as a being. Calling a Resonance costs
MP and burns one bar of the party's own music; while the Resonance
sounds, its effect plays through the active voice.

Mythic frame: Resonances are the surviving spirits of pre-shattering
musicians. When a master died with a song unfinished, sometimes
the song refused to die with them. Those songs gathered into
shapes, learned to listen, and learned to answer when called by
name. Most Resonances live in the Dead Lands now, where there are
fewer voices to compete with.

Baseline MP cost = 4 MP. Cheaper Resonances cost 2; the great
Resonances cost 8.

The eight proposed Resonances:

   1. THE LONG ECHO — tape echo, two-head
      Myth: a wandering singer who could repeat any phrase you
      taught her, but each repetition softer and slightly behind.
      She stopped repeating when there was nothing left to say.
      Effect: the next two attacks by the party are duplicated
      one beat late at 50% damage.
      MP cost: 4

   2. THE MASKED VOICE — vocoder
      Myth: a courtier who could sing in any other person's voice.
      Suno hired and discarded her; her voice became the apparatus
      he uses now. The Resonance is what's left of her own.
      Effect: for one bar, all party damage carries the active
      voice's mode (mode-bonus stacking).
      MP cost: 6

   3. THE SPRING — spring reverb
      Myth: an old hermit who lived in a tin shack and could turn
      any sound into a long shimmering wash. He left his shack one
      morning and the wash kept going.
      Effect: the next heal echoes for two more bars at 25% per
      bar. Stacks weakly with Miel's heal.
      MP cost: 4

   4. THE RING — ring modulator
      Myth: two bell-tuners who married and learned to multiply
      each other's notes. After they died their sound kept folding
      itself into harmonics no one could place.
      Effect: next attack ignores enemy DEF and adds a clangor
      hit at +30% damage. Visually the screen shudders.
      MP cost: 6

   5. THE HEAVY HAND — sidechain compressor
      Myth: a drummer whose strikes were so heavy that every other
      voice in the room ducked out of his way. His hand still
      carries the rhythm somewhere in the world.
      Effect: enemy attacks during the next two bars deal 50%
      damage (their voices duck under the party's downbeats).
      MP cost: 6

   6. THE SCATTER — granular cloud
      Myth: a singer who fell into a bell-mine and was crushed.
      The mine echoed her in tiny shards for a year. She is the
      shards.
      Effect: applies CONFUSE to a single enemy for 4 bars; their
      attack pattern randomizes.
      MP cost: 4

   7. THE SLOW WHEEL — analog phaser
      Myth: a millwright who tuned her wheel to a slow phase and
      it never stopped turning, even after the mill burned.
      Effect: party SPD +1 for the next 4 bars.
      MP cost: 4

   8. THE THREEFOLD — vintage chorus
      Myth: three sisters who sang together so closely no one ever
      knew which was which. One died; the other two kept singing
      for three.
      Effect: party heal-on-cast — every party action this bar also
      heals the caster for 5% HP.
      MP cost: 8

The intent is that Resonances are MEMORABLE rather than balanced.
Players should learn their names and call them by name in their
own heads, the way they remember Bahamut.

-------- CAVE 3 — MIXOLYDIAN SHARD --------

-------- RECURRING THEMES & DEVICES (EXTENDED) --------

For the dossier list, see §RECURRING THEMES. Additions:

   - Shards remember the songs of those who sang to them; touching
     a shard plays back a fragment.
   - Every recovered shard adds one voice to the overworld theme.
     The world gets louder as the party advances.
   - Musicians named in dialogue eventually appear in person —
     no NPC is mentioned without payoff somewhere.
   - Suno's apparatus always speaks before he does; the player
     hears his refineries before they meet him.
   - Pitch is the surface of music here, not the substance — when
     the world goes atonal the music is still there. (Act 3.)
   - Recording is rare and slightly taboo. A recorded voice cannot
     change. Modalians prefer their songs sung live and forgotten.


-------- DESIGN GUARDRAILS --------

Tone rules.
   No winking synth in-jokes. Synthesis is the cosmology, not the
   vocabulary. Characters do not talk about "filters" or "envelopes."
   They talk about voices, breaths, holding tones. The player who
   knows synthesis will hear the parallels; the player who doesn't
   should never feel the joke is on them.

Dialogue rules.
   Slightly antique cadence — closer to translated literature than
   to modern prose. Avoid contemporary idiom. Lines on the norns
   dialogue box must fit ~80 characters per line, max three lines
   on screen. Read every line aloud before keeping it.

Pacing rules.
   This is a tiny screen and a small ensemble. Don't info-dump.
   Show the world by making the player stand near it. A village
   elder can imply a war in two lines if he says them right. The
   game's job is to let players overhear their own story.
